# Depreciation Methods Analysis

## Introduction

Depreciation is the reduction in the value of an asset over time. This project analyzes and compares two commonly used depreciation methods:

* Straight-Line Method
* Diminishing Balance Method

---

## Straight-Line Method

**Formula:**

(Asset Price - Scrap Value) ÷ Life Span

### Results

* Asset Price: **$500,000**
* Annual Depreciation: **$45,000**
* Depreciation Rate: **9%**
* Total Depreciation: **$450,000**
* Final Book Value: **$50,000**

---

## Diminishing Balance Method

**Formula:**

Book Value × Depreciation Rate

### Results

* Depreciation Rate: **20.57%**
* Total Depreciation: **$405,000**
* Final Book Value: **$45,000**

## Key Insights

* Straight-Line Method is suitable for assets with consistent usage over time.
* Diminishing Balance Method better reflects assets that lose value rapidly in the early years.
* Both methods result in significant asset value reduction over the asset's useful life.
* The choice of method depends on business requirements, accounting policies, and tax considerations.

---

## Conclusion

Both depreciation methods have distinct advantages:

* **Straight-Line Method** offers simplicity and consistent expense recognition.
* **Diminishing Balance Method** provides a more realistic representation of asset value decline.

The appropriate method should be selected based on the nature of the asset and organizational financial policies.
